## Why these picks
- **Genre spread**: patriotic rock → classic rock → arena anthem → southern rock → country-patriotic → hard rock finale. Keeps it moving and familiar to a backyard crowd.
- **Built-in sync gifts**: *For Those About to Rock* has literal cannon fire (put those on mortar booms); *Livin' on a Prayer*'s key change is a natural willow-lift; *American Bad Ass* literally name-matches your **Bad Ass Brocade** finale.
- **The fake-out**: a patriotic Toby Keith peak feels like a closer; cutting to silence + siren makes the Kid Rock finale hit twice as hard.

## Sourcing (buy/download the mp3s)
- Songs: buy the mp3s (Amazon Music / iTunes / Qobuz). Get the **explicit** versions where applicable.
- "Let's get ready to rumble" + "It ain't over yet": grab a short announcer/clip or just **record your own** — cleanest and free.
- **Nuclear siren**: free royalty-free downloads at Pixabay or Orange Free Sounds; or use the **Splice** connector for a higher-quality siren/SFX.

## Build guide (free, no Excel/no DAW purchase)
1. Install **Audacity** (free). Import each mp3 onto its own track.
2. Trim each song to the cut listed, lay them end-to-end on the timeline at the section timecodes above.
3. Use **crossfades** at the seams (select the overlap → Effect ▸ Crossfade Tracks) so songs blend; hard-cut only where noted.
4. Drop the announcer clips and the siren on a separate track at 0:00 and 8:55; leave the 8:55–9:20 music silent so the siren stands alone.
5. Match loudness across songs (Effect ▸ Loudness Normalization) so nothing jumps.
6. Export one **WAV/MP3**. That file's timeline is the show clock — confirm the firework Event Times in `cobra.csv` still line up, nudge cues to musical hits, and load the audio into your player.
